అతని father — 1950s, 1960s లో small breweries ని ground మీద get చేయడానికి try చేసిన shortage లేని own experience తో — Jim కి partner ని find చేసుకోమని advise చేసాడు, ఎందుకంటే business start చేయడం lonely గా ఉంటుంది, "full of ups and downs (ఎత్తు పల్లాలు)" — entrepreneurial roller coaster మీద ఒక్కడివే ఉన్నప్పుడు ఇవి digest చేసుకోవడం కష్టం.

His father, with no shortage of his own experience trying to get small breweries off the ground in the 1950s and 1960s, had advised him to find someone to partner with because starting a business is lonely, “full of ups and downs” that are hard to stomach when you’re on the entrepreneurial roller coaster by yourself.

---

ఇరవై-plus years తరువాత Paul Graham కూడా ఇదే advice unequivocally echo చేసాడు: "Startup start చేయడం ఒక్కరికి too hard. మీరు పనంతా self చేయగలిగినా, brainstorm చేయడానికి, dumb decisions నుండి talk you out చేయడానికి, things wrong అయినప్పుడు cheer up చేయడానికి colleagues అవసరం . . . Startup లో low points అంత low గా ఉంటాయి, వాటిని alone bear చేయగలిగే వాళ్ళు చాలా తక్కువ."


It was a piece of advice echoed unequivocally by Paul Graham more than twenty years later, when he wrote, “Starting a startup is too hard for one person. Even if you could do all the work yourself, you need colleagues to brainstorm with, to talk you out of stupid decisions, and to cheer you up when things go wrong . . . The low points in a startup are so low that few could bear them alone.”

Partners just మీ idea ని consumers fickleness నుండి, competitors ruthlessness నుండి, investors scrutiny నుండి, world events uncertainty నుండి survive చేయడంలో మాత్రమే help చేయరు. వాళ్ళు మిమ్మల్ని కూడా survive అవ్వడానికి help చేస్తారు.

Partners don’t just help your idea survive the fickleness of consumers, the ruthlessness of competitors, the scrutiny of investors, or the uncertainty of world events. They help you survive as well.

---

మీ idea ఎప్పుడైనా real business గా మారాలంటే, మీరు alive and kicking గా ఉండాలి. అంటే మీకు మీ partner — మీ co-founder — కూడా అవసరం.

And if your idea is ever to become a real business, it needs you alive and kicking. Which means it needs your partner—your co-founder—too.

Bootstrapping అంటే — billionaires Rolodex flip చేయడానికి లేకపోతే; మీ first company అయితే, మీరు and మీ partner(s) alone వెళ్తున్నారు — అప్పుడు మీరు ఏం చేస్తారు, అది.

Bootstrapping is what you do if you don’t have a Rolodex of billionaires to flip through and ask for money; if it’s your first company and you’re going it alone, just you and your partner(s).

మరింత specifically, bootstrapping అంటే మీ disposal లో ఉన్నది use చేస్తూ, మీరు ఉండాలనుకున్న చోటుకి చేరుకోవడం.

More specifically, bootstrapping is using what you have at your disposal to get yourself where you want to be.

Typically, bootstrapping గురించి మాట్లాడేటప్పుడు, అది financial conversation, and to an extent అది ఎప్పటికీ true. మీ business ని stand up చేయడానికి other people’s money లేకుండా — friends and family నుండి అయినా, venture capital నుండి అయినా, SBA loan లాంటిది అయినా — మీరు things pay చేయడానికి other ways find చేయాలి: credit cards, personal savings, profits ని business లోకి cycling back చేయడం.

Typically, when we talk about bootstrapping, it’s a financial conversation, and to an extent that will always be true. Without other people’s money to stand up your business—whether it’s from friends and family, venture capital, or something like an SBA loan—you have to find other ways to pay for things: credit cards, personal savings, cycling profits back into the business.

But having spoken to hundreds of brilliant entrepreneurs, I know that bootstrapping is more than simply using alternative sources of personal financing as a last resort.

It’s also about keeping control of your business as long as you can.
